%\noindent\textbf{Task Evaluations.} Tables~\ref{tbl_post_behavioural} and~\ref{tbl_post_system} provide the results of the post-task surveys -- for behavioural and system-sided questions, respectively. Survey questions are detailed for reference in Section~\ref{sec:method:experience}, all of which used the seven-point Likert scale for responses (with $7$ indicating strong agreement, and $1$ indicating strong disagreement). Turning our attention first to the behavioural survey results, we see no significant differences across conditions, systems or tasks. However, across all conditions, systems or tasks and questions, subjects broadly agreed with the statements they were presented with, suggesting that they felt successful in completing the search tasks, and completed them quickly. All were in agreement that they carefully checked their documents for relevance/new entities before saving, but were in broad disagreement that they examined a \emph{few} documents per query, indicating that across all conditions, subjects felt as though they had examined more than they felt they needed to originally.

%Regarding the system-sided questions presented in Table~\ref{tbl_post_system}, subjects considered the system offered a reasonably quick and straightforward experience to find results, with a generally positive outcome. The system generally did not appear to be considered cumbersome to use, and subjects did not find the system made it overly difficult to complete the tasks -- a significant difference exists between the two tasks for this question ($F(1, 201=5.51, p<0.05)$). Overall, subjects felt happy with how the system performed, and thus appeared to show some confidence in their decisions.
